SOURCE:
World Health Organization (WHO)
Guideline: Sugars intake for adults and children
https://www.who.int/publications/i/item/9789241549028

AUTHORITY:
World Health Organization (international public health authority)

TOPIC:
Free sugars

CLAIM:
Free sugars should account for less than 10% of total daily energy intake.
Reducing intake below 5% provides additional health benefits.

RATIONALE:
High intake of free sugars is associated with increased risk of overweight,
obesity, and dental caries.
Free sugars contribute energy without providing essential nutrients.

PRACTICAL IMPLICATIONS:
- Limit consumption of sugary drinks, sweets, and desserts
- Prefer whole fruit over fruit juices
- Be cautious with processed foods containing added sugars

LIMITATIONS:
This guideline applies to free sugars and does not include sugars naturally
present in whole fruits, vegetables, and milk.
