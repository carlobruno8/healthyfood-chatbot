AUTHORITY:
World Health Organization (international public health authority)

TOPIC:
Fruits and vegetables

CLAIM:
Adults should consume at least 400 grams (approximately 5 portions) of fruits and vegetables per day.

RATIONALE:
Adequate intake of fruits and vegetables reduces the risk of noncommunicable diseases,
including cardiovascular disease, stroke, and certain cancers.
Fruits and vegetables are important sources of dietary fiber, vitamins
(such as vitamin C, vitamin A, and folate), and minerals.

PRACTICAL IMPLICATIONS:
- Include vegetables in at least two main meals per day
- Consume fruit as snacks or desserts instead of sugary foods
- Aim for a variety of colors across the week to increase micronutrient diversity

LIMITATIONS:
This recommendation applies to healthy adults and does not replace individualized
medical or dietary advice.
