SOURCE:
European Food Safety Authority (EFSA)
Dietary Reference Values for Fibre
EFSA Journal (2015)
https://www.efsa.europa.eu/en/efsajournal/pub/4254

AUTHORITY:
European Food Safety Authority (EU scientific risk assessment body)

TOPIC:
Dietary fiber

CLAIM:
An adequate intake of dietary fiber for adults is 25 grams per day.

RATIONALE:
Adequate fiber intake supports normal bowel function and is associated with
reduced risk of cardiovascular disease, type 2 diabetes, and colorectal cancer.
Fiber contributes to satiety and overall dietary quality.

PRACTICAL IMPLICATIONS:
- Include whole grains such as oats, whole wheat, and brown rice
- Consume legumes (beans, lentils, chickpeas) regularly
- Increase intake of vegetables, fruits, nuts, and seeds

LIMITATIONS:
This value represents an adequate intake for the general adult population
and may vary depending on individual energy needs and health conditions.
